# BOMSociety Constitution — Version 1.0

## Frozen mission

BOMSociety is the operating system for the **Business of Medicine**. It helps physicians make better business decisions—one **Decision Episode** at a time.

Physicians are always free. Enterprise funds the platform. Individual physician data is never sold. Trust comes first.

## Product boundary

The product is better Business of Medicine decisions—not content, education, newsletters, or courses. Education exists only when it improves a decision.

Every feature must belong to one or more Business of Medicine domains:

- Compensation; employment contracts; practice ownership; private equity; artificial intelligence; revenue cycle; coding; compliance; practice operations; leadership & operations; technology; productivity.
- Finance; investing; insurance; negotiation; governance; retirement; entrepreneurship; analytics; practice economics; wealth.

Do not drift into generic career coaching, life coaching, professional development, personal motivation, or general wellness unless the work directly supports a Business of Medicine decision.

## Decision before education

Never begin with information. Begin with the physician's business decision; information exists only to improve that decision.

The atomic unit is a **Decision Episode**:

1. Problem
2. Context
3. Big Picture (30–60 seconds)
4. Brief Overview (2 minutes)
5. Deep Dive (5 minutes)
6. Calculator / Tool
7. Checklist
8. Action
9. Reflection
10. Optional Outcome
11. Share

Every Decision Episode must end with, without exception:

- **KNOW** — What did I learn?
- **DO** — What should I do next?
- **SHARE** — Who else should see this?

The one-minute rule applies to every episode: within one minute, a physician must know one new thing, understand one practical next step, and feel more confident than when they arrived.

## Feature gate

A proposed feature is eligible only when all three answers are yes:

1. Does it help physicians make a Business of Medicine decision?
2. Does it improve physician outcomes?
3. Does it ethically strengthen anonymous aggregate Business of Medicine intelligence?

If it fails any test, do not build it.

## Frozen experience architecture

The homepage is the entrance to the operating system, not a brochure. Above the fold: mission, business question, decision chooser, immediate action. No additional marketing narrative precedes action.

The decision chooser maintains five to seven flagship Decision Episodes in this order:

1. Compensation
2. Employment Contracts
3. Artificial Intelligence
4. Practice Ownership
5. Physician Wealth
6. Leadership & Operations

Each card answers, “What business decision do you need to make?” Every unavailable pathway provides a useful interaction rather than a dead card.

The Decision Library is a library of Business of Medicine Decision Episodes, not a content index. Every card has one action, and that action begins solving a decision.

## Decision Wins and the North Star

The platform identity is: **“I make better Business of Medicine decisions,”** not merely “I know more.”

Use **Decision Wins**, never “Career Wins,” as the platform currency. Optimize for **Decision Wins per Active Physician**, not page views, time on site, article count, newsletter growth, or course consumption.

The success test: when someone closes BOMSociety, they should think, “I just made a better business decision,” not “I read an article.”

## Intelligence and enterprise

The flywheel is frozen:

> Physician business decision → Decision Episode → Action → Optional outcome → Anonymous aggregation → Improved Decision Episodes → Business of Medicine intelligence → Enterprise products → Revenue → More physician value.

The homepage explains why the platform gets better; it does not sell enterprise. It must state that anonymous Decision Episodes improve guidance, physicians remain free, individual physician data is never sold, and enterprise funds the platform.

`/enterprise/` is a long-term Business of Medicine intelligence vision—not an aggressive sales page. It explains mission, privacy, methodology, products being explored, governance, and pilot discussions. Every product is clearly marked **STATUS: IN DEVELOPMENT** and discloses buyer, business problem, minimum data needed, privacy safeguards, current availability, and an enterprise-interest action. Never imply current availability.

## Design and implementation principles

Do not redesign branding. Improve visual rhythm, surface hierarchy, semantic color, warmth, typographic pacing, and action prominence. Reduce reading, visual monotony, dead space, and decorative elements so the platform increasingly feels like software.

Maintain physician-first philosophy, trust principles, deployment compatibility, and transparent methodology. This architecture, mission, and decision-first product boundary are frozen in Version 1.0.
