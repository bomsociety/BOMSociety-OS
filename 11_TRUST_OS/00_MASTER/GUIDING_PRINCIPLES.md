# Guiding Principles

1. **Privacy by architecture:** collect the minimum, separate identifiers from events, and aggregate before enterprise access.
2. **Evidence over assertion:** confidence reflects source, freshness, corroboration, and integrity—not self-claim alone.
3. **Zero trust:** authenticate and authorize every request, workload, and data flow; network location confers no trust.
4. **Fail safely:** uncertainty reduces privileges and exportability; it never creates a hidden approval.
5. **Composable controls:** providers, jurisdictions, policy rules, and models are replaceable adapters behind versioned contracts.
6. **Observable accountability:** decision inputs, policy version, actor, and outcome are append-only audit events.
7. **Purpose limitation:** data use must be bound to declared purpose, consent/legal basis, retention, and recipient.
8. **Human oversight:** automated enforcement has escalation paths, measured error rates, and appeal procedures.
