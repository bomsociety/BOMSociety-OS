# BTOS Trust Constitution

## Purpose and authority
BTOS protects people, institutions, and the integrity of BOMSociety's Decision Intelligence. Product teams may extend controls but may not weaken these obligations without an approved architecture decision record, Privacy/Security review, and documented accountable owner.

## Rights and commitments
- People control personal data through understandable notice, consent where required, access, correction, deletion, and objection channels.
- Verification is evidence-based, minimally invasive, expiring, and contestable; a badge is never a blanket authorization.
- Decisions affecting access, eligibility, or risk are explainable to the affected party at an appropriate level and support review and appeal.
- Enterprise buyers receive only contracted, aggregated outputs with suppression, provenance summaries, and methodology.
- Employees, services, and AI agents receive only the permissions necessary for a bounded purpose and duration.

## Control hierarchy
Law and regulator requirements → contract/data-use agreement → this Constitution → approved ADR → standards/runbooks → implementation. The stricter applicable control wins.

## Accountability
The Board delegates program oversight to the executive owner. The CISO owns security controls; Privacy Officer owns data rights; Trust Council owns scoring policy; Data Steward owns dataset use; system owners own operation. Material exceptions have an owner, expiration, compensating control, and quarterly review.
