# Business of Medicine Intelligence Architecture

## Permanent flow

> Decision Episode → exactly one Intelligence Platform → anonymous aggregation → validation → improved physician guidance → future enterprise deliverable

The physician decision is the starting point. Content is a decision-support artifact, not a generic topic stream. No Episode enters the library without one named Intelligence Platform.

## Required Decision Episode mapping

Every Decision Episode maps **1:1 to exactly one Intelligence Platform**. Secondary platform mappings are not permitted.

## Content quality gate

An Episode must improve a physician's decision—not teach because a topic exists. It is original physician-specific guidance distilled from credible public thinking without copying or generic summarization. Its permanent modes are **BIG PICTURE · 30 SEC** for recognition, **BRIEF OVERVIEW · 2 MIN** for understanding, and **DEEP DIVE · 5 MIN** for execution. The three modes use an insight and action, a framework and checklist, then practical tools, questions, references, reflection, and outcome planning.

## Intelligence platform contract

All nine permanent Intelligence Platforms are **IN DEVELOPMENT**. Before a platform can produce an enterprise deliverable, it must define its primary Business of Medicine domain, enterprise buyer, deliverable, physician value, feeding Episode, minimum aggregation and validation requirements, privacy safeguards, and development status.

Global minimums include a documented taxonomy, anonymous signals, cohort suppression below the documented threshold, time-window sufficiency, validation checks, and a methodology/limitations statement. Individual physician data is never sold; patient data, raw documents, and identifiable profiles are excluded.
